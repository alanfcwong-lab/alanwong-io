Methodology

How this tool works and where the framework comes from

The 2x2 Impact vs. Effort matrix is a standard strategic prioritization framework used by consulting firms, product teams, and operations leaders. Initiatives are scored on two dimensions (1–10 each) and plotted into four quadrants: Quick Wins (high impact, low effort — do first), Big Bets (high impact, high effort — plan carefully), Fill-ins (low impact, low effort — delegate or batch), and Deprioritize (low impact, high effort — cut or defer).

Roadmap sequencing is driven by the impact-to-effort ratio within each quadrant. Initiatives with a higher ratio (more impact per unit of effort) are scheduled to start earlier. This means two Big Bets don't automatically share the same timeline — one with impact 9 / effort 6 (ratio 1.5) starts before one with impact 6 / effort 9 (ratio 0.67). The matrix position acts as a continuous priority score, not just a quadrant bucket. Dragging an initiative to a new position recalculates the entire roadmap in real time.

Year ranges by quadrant: Quick Wins start in 2026 (execute immediately). Big Bets are staggered across 2026–2028 based on ratio ranking — best-ratio bets start earliest. Fill-ins spread across 2026–2027. Deprioritized items are pushed to 2029–2030. All year assignments update dynamically when you reposition initiatives on the matrix.

The YVR Operational Strategy & Reporting preset is structured around the mandate of YVR's Director of Operational Strategy and Reporting — designing a comprehensive reporting ecosystem, advancing digital transformation, driving cross-functional alignment, and strengthening operational performance measurement. Initiative names reflect publicly known YVR priorities; impact and effort scores are illustrative, not sourced from YVR's internal planning.
